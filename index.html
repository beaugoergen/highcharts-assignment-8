GDP in relation to exports

by Beau Goergen

The World Bank data

The United States GDP has been steadily rising around 4% on average over the last 10 years. To this day, the United States remains the world's largest economy in terms of nominal GDP. Although the USA has the highest GDP globally, it sits at eigth for highest GDP per capita -- Luxembourg being number one. The United States GDP is expected to continue to grow through strategic trade deals and high productivity.

International Trade Administration data

The United States is one of the leading exporters of goods and services in the world. To the surprise of many, the United States exports the majority of its goods and services to Canada. This is mainly due to geograpihcal access. The United States historically has a trade deficit for goods and a trade surplus for services. The United States remains the second-largest trading country in the world.
